An ergonomic mouse can be the difference between a comfortable workday and a nagging wrist ache, but you don’t need to spend a lot to get relief. The right shape encourages a neutral hand posture, reduces muscle strain, and often adds custom buttons that speed up your workflow. The challenge is that “ergonomic” covers everything from vertical grips to sculpted ambidextrous designs, so focusing on your hand size and grip style matters more than the price tag. For this round-up we looked at the most consistently recommended ergonomic mice under various budgets. We prioritized models that have stood up well in owner reviews and expert tests for comfort, button feel, and tracking accuracy. Whether you want a full vertical reorientation or just a more contoured shape, these three picks cover the most common needs without breaking the bank.

Best for specific needs

Best for first-time vertical mouse users on a budget

If you’ve never tried a vertical mouse and aren’t ready to spend much, the Anker 2.4G Vertical Ergonomic Mouse is your safest bet. It gives you the full 60-degree grip angle common in pricier vertical mice, and its sensor works on almost any surface. Thousands of positive owner reviews confirm that it’s a reliable introduction to ergonomic computing without the sticker shock. Our pick: Anker 2.4G Vertical Ergonomic Mouse.

Best for power users who need multi-device control

The Logitech MX Vertical excels when you work across a desktop, laptop, and tablet. Its Bluetooth and receiver support three paired devices, and the Logitech Flow software lets you move your cursor between them as if they were one display. The custom buttons can be mapped to common tasks, saving you time and hand motion. Our pick: Logitech MX Vertical.

Best for small hands and quiet workspaces

The Logitech M330 Silent Plus is the ergonomic mouse for people with smaller hands who need to keep noise to a minimum. Its sculpted shape encourages a natural resting position, and the silent clicks won’t disturb coworkers or family members. It’s also one of the lightest ergonomic mice available, making it easy to carry between home and office. Our pick: Logitech M330 Silent Plus.

Our verdict

Choosing an ergonomic mouse doesn’t have to be overwhelming. If you’re new to the category and working with a limited budget, the Anker 2.4G Vertical gives you the biggest ergonomic benefit for the least money. For those who want the best build and multi-device flexibility, the Logitech MX Vertical is worth every extra cent. And if your hands are on the smaller side or you just need a quiet, compact companion, the Logitech M330 Silent Plus is a fantastic low-cost solution. Any of these three will reduce strain more than a standard mouse, pick the one that matches your hand size, workspace, and wallet.
