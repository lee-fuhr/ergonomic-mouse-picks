Best ergonomic mice for 2026

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

The Logitech MX Vertical is our top pick for most people, thanks to its natural handshake grip and solid build quality that helps reduce wrist strain.

If you need a smaller, more travel-friendly design, the Logitech Lift is a great runner-up; for budget shoppers, the Anker Wireless Vertical Mouse offers reliable performance at a very accessible price.

Spending hours clicking and scrolling with a traditional mouse can put unnecessary strain on your wrist and forearm, leading to discomfort over time. Ergonomic mice are designed to promote a more neutral hand position, reducing the risk of repetitive strain injuries and making long work sessions far more comfortable. When choosing an ergonomic mouse, think about your grip style (vertical versus contoured), hand size (small, medium, or large), and whether you need wireless connectivity, extra programmable buttons, or a rechargeable battery. The best choice depends on your specific needs, not just on feature lists.

Best for specific needs

Best for vertical grip lovers

If you are specifically looking for a mouse that tilts your hand into a handshake position, the most effective way to reduce wrist strain, the Logitech MX Vertical is the gold standard. Its 57‑degree angle and high‑quality sensor make it comfortable for all‑day work, and it’s backed by years of positive owner feedback. Our pick: Logitech MX Vertical.

Best for travel and small desks

For those who need ergonomic comfort on the go, the Logitech M720 Triathlon offers excellent battery life and multi‑device switching in a slim, portable package. Its contoured design still provides good support for smaller hands, and silent clicks keep it office‑friendly. Our pick: Logitech M720 Triathlon.

Best for Windows power users

If your workflow revolves around Windows shortcuts and you value a sculpted, palm‑filling shape, the Microsoft Sculpt Ergonomic Mouse is a top choice. Its dedicated Windows button and thumb rest make navigation seamless, and the unique dome design encourages a relaxed hand position. Our pick: Microsoft Sculpt Ergonomic Mouse.

Our verdict

Ultimately, the best ergonomic mouse is the one that fits your hand, your work habits, and your lifestyle. For most people, the Logitech MX Vertical delivers an unbeatable balance of comfort, build quality, and performance. If your hands are smaller or your budget is tighter, the Logitech Lift and Anker Wireless Vertical Mouse are excellent alternatives. For a premium contoured experience, the Microsoft Sculpt Ergonomic Mouse is hard to beat on a Windows setup, while the compact Logitech M720 Triathlon is perfect for travel. Take a moment to consider your grip and daily tasks, any of these picks will be a significant upgrade over a standard mouse.
